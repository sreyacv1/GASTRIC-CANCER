We wish to submit our original research manuscript, **"A multi-omics analysis of
gastric cancer identifies an externally-validated stromal/fibroblast prognostic
programme, with cautionary tumour-microbiome and Mendelian-randomisation
assessments,"** for consideration in ⟦JOURNAL NAME⟧.

Prognostic stratification of gastric cancer beyond TNM stage remains an open
problem, and the literature contains many transcriptomic signatures and tumour-
microbiome "dysbiosis" claims that are reported on single cohorts without
leakage control, external replication, or batch-confounding checks. Our study
was designed to do the opposite: to identify the biology underlying gastric-
cancer prognosis and then to test it — rigorously and honestly — against
independent data.

Our principal findings are:

1. **An externally-validated stromal/cancer-associated-fibroblast (CAF)
   programme** is the reproducible transcriptomic biology underlying gastric-
   cancer prognosis. Its co-expression module is strongly preserved across three
   independent cohorts (module-preservation Zsummary 15.9–17.1) and localises
   unambiguously to fibroblasts in single-cell data (43,992 cells).

2. **A 25-gene signature operationalises this programme honestly.** Under
   leakage-free nested cross-validation its discrimination is a modest Harrell
   C = 0.61 (not the optimistic apparent 0.72), it validates in two of three
   external cohorts (total n = 922), and a conservative meta-analysis is
   non-significant (pooled HR 1.19, 95% CI 0.96–1.47). We report this modest,
   time-limited performance transparently rather than overstating it.

3. **Two cautionary negative arms.** A tumour-microbiome classifier that appears
   strong is shown to be a sequencing-batch artefact, and Mendelian
   randomisation finds no causal microbial effect on gastric-cancer risk. We
   present these as an honest, multi-cohort demonstration that batch confounding
   and comparator choice — not a fixed dysbiotic signature — dominate low-biomass
   tissue-microbiome results.

We believe this work suits ⟦JOURNAL NAME⟧ because its value lies in
**methodological rigour, external validation, and transparent reporting of
